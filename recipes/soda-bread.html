Irish Soda Bread

This classic, quick bread uses baking soda and buttermilk instead of yeast to achieve its rise. Its inexpensive and easy-to-find ingredients make this recipe doable even on a tight budget.

When you bake this warm, crumbly loaf, you'll have trouble leaving some for later!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup margarine, softened
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 egg
  • 4 tbsp white sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted (optional)
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Farenheit and lightly grease a large baking sheet.
  2. Mix the flour, margarine, sugar, baking soda, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl. Stir in one cup of buttermilk and egg.
  3. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead slightly. Form dough into a round and place on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Optionally, combine 1/4th cup of melted butter and 1/4th cup of buttermilk in a small bowl, then brush over the surface of the loaf. Cut an 'X' into the top of the loaf with a sharp knife.
  5. Bake in preheated oven for about 45 to 50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Serve with butter and jelly or other toppings as desired.
